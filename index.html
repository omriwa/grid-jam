Plain Language Explanation

How EPI Works — Simply

Drones and missiles use GPS to know exactly where they are and where they're going. Without GPS, they're flying blind in the final kilometers — and instead of hitting a precise target, they miss by tens or hundreds of meters.

Grid-Jam's EPI system places a network of powerful jammers around the infrastructure you want to protect. When a threat approaches, those jammers flood the area with interference signals — corrupting the GPS signal the weapon relies on. The result: a precision weapon becomes a statistical one.

Technical Operation

System Architecture

EPI uses two classes of jamming units — omnidirectional for drone/swarm threats in the final approach phase, and vertical directional for ballistic and hypersonic missiles during atmospheric descent.

▸ Mode 01 / Short-Range

Omnidirectional Grid

A ring of omnidirectional jammers ensures complete angular coverage, defeating 4+ element CRPA antennas for at least the last 5km of the threat's flight path to the target.

  • Defeats CRPA antennas (4+ elements)
  • 360° jamming coverage, no null zones
  • Effective against drone swarms
  • Minimum 5km continuous jamming
  • Activates on alert or manually
▸ Mode 02 / Long-Range

Vertical Directional Array

High-power vertical directional jammers deployed in a grouped array provide continuous GNSS disruption across approximately 50km of atmospheric entry — covering the entire terminal descent phase of ballistic and hypersonic weapons.

  • ~50km vertical jamming range
  • Covers full ballistic descent phase
  • Effective against hypersonic threats
  • Yagi antenna configuration
  • Remote activation capability
▸ Mode 03 / Integration

Command & Control Integration

EPI integrates seamlessly with national air defense warning systems. It can be triggered automatically upon radar alert, remotely by operators, or manually at the local unit level — providing flexible response across all threat scenarios.

  • Integrates with existing C2 systems
  • Auto-activation on air defense alert
  • Remote on/off via UHF radio link
  • Adjustable jamming duration (5–30 min)
  • Country-wide scalable deployment
▸ Mode 04 / Deployment

Rapid Field Deployment

EPI units are designed for pre-planned rapid deployment in days. Units can be mounted on vehicles for mobility, solving energy supply and security challenges in open terrain. Planning and testing occur in advance; units are stored until operational need.

  • Country-wide deployment in days
  • Vehicle-mounted option available
  • 12–28V DC power (portable)
  • Weather-resistant outdoor casing
  • ~5–8kg per unit for portability

Frequently Asked Questions

Technical & Operational FAQ

Key questions from government procurement officers, defense program managers, and military planners.

Does EPI shoot down drones and missiles, or does it work differently? +

EPI does not physically intercept any threat. Instead, it continuously jams GNSS signals across wide areas, particularly around strategic infrastructure. GNSS-guided munitions, drones and missiles lose their last-kilometer precision and effectively miss the target's critical points. The hit becomes statistical rather than surgical — often a few tens or hundreds of meters off, which can mean the difference between destruction and survival of the asset.

Why can't a single high-power jammer protect a site? +

Modern military drones like the Shahed-136 carry CRPA (Controlled Reception Pattern Antennas) with 4 or more elements. A 4-element CRPA can defeat up to 3 jammers from different directions by electronically steering nulls toward interference sources. EPI deploys 4 or more jammer units surrounding the protected area — overwhelming even advanced CRPA systems by flooding all incoming angles simultaneously. This is why the grid/array architecture is the core innovation.

How does EPI integrate with existing national air defense? +

Every country has an air defense warning and command system. EPI units can be integrated with these systems for automatic activation upon air defense alert — locally and country-wide. They can also be manually activated locally when there is information of imminent attacks or upon first explosions. The system is designed to operate as a seamless additional layer to existing kinetic air defense, not as a replacement for it.

How quickly can EPI be deployed across an entire country? +

EPI jamming units are designed for wartime deployment. They are typically not deployed before immediate need. The deployment plan and site positions are mapped and tested in advance, with equipment stored until needed. Full deployment — including many jammer nodes covering an entire country's critical infrastructure sites — can be accomplished in several days. Vehicle-mounted options solve energy supply and security challenges for open-terrain sites. A country-wide network is achievable within a reasonable budget.

Can EPI protect civilian populations and entire cities? +

EPI is designed specifically to protect critical national infrastructure assets — power generation, oil & gas, seaports, airports, military installations, and government facilities. It is not intended to create a blanket shield over entire cities or protect individual structures. In wartime, prioritization of strategic assets is essential. EPI's value is in protecting the infrastructure whose loss would be catastrophic to state function and national resilience.

What are the export regulations for EPI systems? +

GNSS jamming equipment is included in the Wassenaar Arrangement Munitions List as item ML 11.b: "Satellite navigation system jamming equipment and specially designed components." As such, export is regulated by the exporting country and requires a defense equipment export license. Grid-Jam works with customers through the appropriate government-to-government and commercial licensing channels. Please contact us directly to discuss procurement pathways for your country or organization.

Has EPI been evaluated against real-world threats like the Shahed-136? +

Yes. Grid-Jam systems were evaluated and approved as effective against GNSS-guided drones including the Shahed-136 platform — one of the most widely deployed and combat-proven GPS-guided kamikaze drones in current use. The Shahed-136 features a 4-element CRPA anti-jam antenna, making it resistant to standard single-point jamming. EPI's grid architecture was specifically designed to defeat this class of target.
