Solution Overview

What Is EPI?
Electronic Protection of Infrastructure

EPI is a geographically distributed array of RF jamming units that work together as a synchronized grid. Think of it as an electronic minefield — invisible, continuously active, covering all approach vectors around a protected asset.

When a GPS-guided weapon enters the EPI zone, it loses precise positioning. A strike that would have landed within 2–5 meters of a target is deflected by tens to hundreds of meters — turning a surgical blow into a statistical miss.

Technical Overview

How Grid-Jam Works

A plain-language explanation of the technology — from the threat to the solution.

  • 1
    GPS is the Weapon's Brain
    Modern drones and missiles use GPS (a type of GNSS) to know exactly where they are and navigate to their target with meter-level precision. Disrupt that GPS signal and the weapon loses its ability to hit a specific building, tank, or facility.
  • 2
    The Problem: CRPA Antennas Resist Simple Jamming
    Military-grade drones and missiles carry CRPA (Controlled Reception Pattern Antennas) with 4 or more elements. These smart antennas identify and cancel out jamming signals from up to 3 directions simultaneously — rendering single or triple jammer deployments ineffective against modern guided munitions.
  • 3
    The Solution: A Grid That Overwhelms CRPA
    Grid-Jam deploys many jammer units arranged in a geometric array surrounding the protected site. Because jammers attack from more directions than the CRPA can nullify simultaneously, the weapon's guidance system is overwhelmed and loses lock on GPS satellites.
  • 4
    From Precision to Statistics
    Once inside the EPI zone, the weapon's position error grows from meters to tens or hundreds of meters. A strike that would have destroyed an oil pipeline or power transformer now misses entirely — the infrastructure survives.
  • 5
    Coverage for All Threat Types
    Omnidirectional low-altitude jammers cover drones and cruise missiles for the last 5+ km of approach. High-power vertical long-range directional arrays cover ballistic missile trajectories over ~50 km — including hypersonic glide vehicles during atmospheric re-entry.
  • 6
    Integrated Into Your Defense Ecosystem
    EPI can be switched on/off manually or triggered automatically by integration with national air-defense warning systems. It adds a zero-interceptor-cost defensive layer alongside existing radar or Iron Dome-class systems, or as a standalone last line of defense.

Historical Evidence

Drone & Missile Attack
Case Studies

Every major conflict since 2019 has validated the threat. These are the real-world events that shaped Grid-Jam's mission and demonstrate the urgency of GNSS-denial defense.

2019
Energy Infrastructure
Houthi Drone Strike on Saudi ARAMCO
Iranian-supplied drones and cruise missiles struck Saudi Arabia's Abqaiq and Khurais oil processing facilities, temporarily cutting 5% of global oil supply and causing estimated damages exceeding $5 billion USD. The attack demonstrated that GNSS-guided drones could precisely target the soft spots of hardened industrial infrastructure at scale.
2020
Military Infrastructure
Azerbaijan–Armenia: Israeli Drones Defeat Air Defense
Azerbaijan deployed Harop and Bayraktar TB2 drones guided by GPS to systematically destroy Armenia's entire radar network and anti-air infrastructure. The conflict proved that relatively low-cost precision drones could eliminate conventional air-defense systems — rendering expensive ground-based radar networks obsolete without GNSS-denial capability.
2022–
Energy & National Infrastructure
Russia–Ukraine: Industrial-Scale Drone and Missile Campaign
Russia deployed the Iranian Shahed-136 (later produced domestically as Geran-2) in swarms of hundreds per attack, targeting Ukrainian power plants, substations, water treatment facilities, and railway infrastructure. Ukraine lost the majority of its energy infrastructure to these attacks. Interceptor systems proved insufficient to intercept low, slow-flying drones at scale, and interceptor stockpiles were rapidly depleted.
2023–
Military & Civilian Targets
Hezbollah Drones Penetrate Israeli Air Defense
Hezbollah drones flew low-altitude profiles that evaded Iron Dome detection, striking military installations and civilian targets including a documented hit near the Israeli Prime Minister's residence. Israel's oil refinery and a major hospital were struck by precise Iranian ballistic missiles. The conflict demonstrated that even the world's most advanced multi-layer air-defense architecture has exploitable gaps against GPS-guided low-altitude munitions.
